Honours Research Project Tenerife Field Course Mathematical Biology And Ecology
Extended research project under the supervision of Professor Dave Hodgson in modelling plant life history dynamics using population projection matrices. Ecology and Evolution of Disease Marine Spacial Ecology
Through various historical and present day examples you will learn the principles underlying the ecology and evolution of infectious diseases as well as the theory describing these processes. This will be complemented by computer practicals where you will use the open software platform R to simulate disease outbreaks, design effective control strategies and study the evolution of drug resistance. More infomation This module will provide training in fundamental and applied principles of marine spatial data collection, analyses, management and interpretation. Using a range of practicals and fieldwork you will be trained to become an early-stage spatial data practitioner. Practical sessions will provide training, ranging from simple approaches to present and analyse spatial data through to complex techniques for manipulating and mapping ocean remote sensing and biologging data. Second Year

Iceland Field Course Mathematics of the Environment Analysis of Biological Data
The Iceland field course will be conducted as a student-led research expedition to explore how temperature variation shapes the structure and carbon cycling of freshwater ecosystems. We will use the unique geothermally-heated catchment of the Hveragerdi valley as a natural experiment, which within the same 1 km2 area, holds streams that range in temperature from 5 to 50C. We will investigate how adaptation to different temperature regimes impacts on the metabolism of the stream microbial communities. More infomation Using Cornwall’s living laboratory as a source of inspiration for your mathematical investigations, in each topic of study, you will embark on a field trip to a local site or activity of direct relevance to environmental and sustainability. The field trips are an essential component of the module and serve a number of purposes. Firstly, they will give you first-hand experience of the underlying scientific and societal issues, and, secondly, you will consolidate your own data sets to be used now and in later stages of your programme. More infomation The aim of this module is to provide training in the collection and analysis of biological datasets, recognising that statistics is a tool for understanding data and not an end in itself. The module is based around understanding the logic behind statistical tests and applying them using the open-access statistics program ‘R’. More infomation


Biodiversity and Conservation Biology of Aquatic Vertebrates Ecological Consultancy
This module introduces the study of biodiversity and conservation biology, and emphasises their growing importance. There is an emphasis on pattern and process, and theory is backed up by case studies from various biological systems. The module content is driven by the current global need to describe, value and prioritise biodiversity under threat and understand the fundamental issues underpinning conservation strategies. More infomation The aim of the module is to provide you with an in-depth insight into the range of adaptations for life in the water and the ways in which biology has adapted to cope with the challenges of low oxygen, high pressure and unpredictability in resources. This insight will help you to develop your critical and creative thinking about how life has adapted to deal with specific challenges and provide key employability skills in analysis and communication. More infomation This module deals with the key issues and techniques involved in terrestrial ecological consultancy; it is therefore excellent preparation for those who intend to pursue a career in this sector or those who would like to understand more about the kind of work this entails. The module will give you an overview of protected species legislation in the UK and outline the ecology and survey methodology of key protected species (including bats, otters, badgers, dormice, water voles and great crested newt). The module also aims to provide experience of conducting Phase 1 habitat surveys and desk studies. More infomation
